Research

Primary areas of research

My research broadly centers on leveraging molecular information (most commonly next-generation sequencing data) to understand the diversification of species, metapopulations, and populations through time and across the landscape, and in translating these data to real-world problems involving the conservation and management of biodiversity. This necessitates the parsing of data from various "-omics" sources through the respective lenses of both population genetic and phylogenetic methodologies, and often necessitating novel computational tools and pipelines. As such, I have a broad interest in the hierarchical evolution of subpopulations to species and in the molecular and computational methods developed to examine it.
